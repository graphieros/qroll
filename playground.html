Carousel components

Qroll allows you to create carousel components. Anywhere inside a slide, just drop a css class on a div and voilà.


<div id="qroll-parent" class="qroll-main qroll-loop qroll-tooltip">
    <div id="mySlide1" class="my-slide-class">
      <h1> Slide 1 </h1>
      <div class="qroll-carousel-component-horizontal">         <div>Horizontal slide 1</div>
        <div>Horizontal slide 2</div>
      </div>
    </div>
</div>

Qroll handles dialog elements for you. You just have to provide an id, a class, and optional data attributes. Place your dialog div at the same level as your slides. You can also provide your own css classes to design your dialog element the way you want.


<div id="qroll-parent" class="qroll-main qroll-loop qroll-tooltip">
    <div><h1> Slide 1 </h1></div>
    <div>
      <h1> Slide 2 </h1>
      <button onclick="qroll.openDialog('myId')"> Say hi </button>
    </div>

    <div id="myId" class="qroll-dialog" data-title="My Title" data-css-classes="my-dialog-class-1 my-dialog-class-2">
      Hello, World!
    </div>
</div>

You can set up a carousel inside of your dialog.
Just insert a div inside like so: <div data-carousel> [add your slides] </div>
If you want this carousel to auto slide, just add a data attribute:
<div data-carousel data-auto-slide="true"> [add your slides] </div>
